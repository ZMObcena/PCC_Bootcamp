# Umbra
The game takes place in an abandoned mansion. The player is an investigative journalist who recently received a tip from an anonymous source about a series of unexplained phenomena linked to the mansion. The owner was rumored to made a significant discovery that was lost when he vanished mysteriously decades ago. The player's goal is to investigate these claims and uncover the truth behind his disappearance. The player has to find and use scattered light switches and levers to reveal hidden areas or mechanisms that aid in the progression and solve  puzzles related to the mansion’s secrets and the owner’s research. 

The hand-crank flashlight introduces a unique gameplay element, requiring players to manually generate light. The use of light switches and levers that provide only brief illumination enhances the game’s urgency and challenge. Players must plan their movements and actions to make the most of these light sources. The mechanics of sound sensitivity and light repulsion create a cat-and-mouse dynamic with the monster. The player must carefully navigate their environment and manage their noise levels to stay safe.

This game is for fans of survival horror. Players who enjoy a blend of suspense, strategy, and survival elements will find the game engaging. The tension of managing light and sound while evading a monster creates a thrilling experience. This is also for puzzle enthusiasts. Those who appreciate puzzles and environmental challenges will enjoy the game’s focus on solving mysteries and uncovering hidden secrets within the mansion.

# Gameplay Highlights
* The player has a hand-crank flashlight. It doesn't need batteries but requires the player to manually crank it to generate light.
* The monster is afraid of the light. Shining the flashlight directly at it would make the monster retreat.
* The monster is highly sensitive to sound. If you make too much sound by running, knocking over objects, or interacting with certain items (i.e. cranking the flashlight) — it will be drawn to your location.
* Scattered around the area are light switches and levers that temporarily illuminate areas or activate mechanisms. However, these light switches only stay on for a short duration.
